SHOPRITE

The Shoprite Group of Companies (JSE: SHP), is Africa's largest food retailer.[2] It operates more than 2,892 stores[3] in 14 countries across Africa.[4] The company's headquarters are in Brackenfell in the Western Cape province of South Africa. Shoprite Holdings Limited is a public company listed on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange, with secondary listings on both the Namibian[5] and Zambian Stock Exchanges.[6] As of 2021, the Shoprite Group employed more than 140,000 people in more than 2,892 stores across the African continent.[7]

The Shoprite Group of companies was established in 1979. In 1990 Shoprite opened in Namibia. In 1991, it acquired the national Checkers chain. In 1995 the first store in Lusaka, Zambia, was opened. That same year they acquired a centralised distribution company Sentra, which had been acting as a central buyer for 550 owner-managed supermarkets, thereby allowing Shoprite to expand into franchising.[7]

Shoprite Holdings Limited comprises the following entities: The Shoprite Checkers Supermarket Group, which consists of: 699 Shoprite supermarkets; 236 Checkers Supermarkets; 38 Checkers Hypers; 434 Usave stores; 432 Furniture outlets (including OK Furniture, OK Power Express, House & Home) 145 MediRite pharmacies and 541 LiquorShops. It has a ticketing business, Computicket, which operates from counters within Group stores and various free-standing outlets. Through its OK Franchise Division, the Group procures and distributes stock to: OK MiniMark convenience stores; OK Foods supermarkets; OK Grocer stores; Megasave wholesale stores; OK Value stores; Sentra stores and buying partners, Friendly supermarkets; Friendly Liquor stores; OK Express; OK Liquor stores.
